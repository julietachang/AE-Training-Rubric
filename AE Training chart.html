Shining 3D Dental · Applications Engineering

Mock Training Evaluation Rubric

Formal assessment of an AE's ability to independently train customers on the Elite IOS, HACS workflow, Ortho/Restoration/Implant workflows, and printer software. This rubric evaluates training capability — not task completion.

Version 2.0

June 2026

Elite · HACS · Restoration
Implant · Ortho · Printer

Purpose of this Assessment This evaluation is NOT simply checking whether the AE can complete a workflow. It measures whether the AE possesses the product knowledge depth, workflow rationale, troubleshooting ability, customer communication skills, and instructional capability required to independently train customers. The AE should demonstrate they can teach — not just perform.
5
Expert
Customer-ready trainer. Teaches with depth, handles any question, adapts to all levels.
4
Proficient
Solid knowledge. Minor gaps or hesitations but recovers well. Close to independent.
3
Developing
Understands basics but needs coaching on depth, rationale, or edge cases.
2
Gaps
Significant knowledge gaps. Would mislead or confuse customers without supervision.
1
Unsatisfactory
Incorrect, unsafe, or unable to perform. Not ready for any customer interaction.

Section 5 · Cross-Cutting Trainer Competencies
Communication, adaptability, critical thinking, troubleshooting, professionalism

CriterionObservable BehaviorsScore (1–5)Evaluator Notes
Teaches vs. Clicks
Explains the purpose, rationale, and outcome of each step — not just executes it silently.
▲ Excellent
Every action is accompanied by a clear "because…" statement; pauses to ensure customer understanding before proceeding.
● Meets Expectations
Narrates most steps; occasional silence on routine actions.
▼ Needs Improvement
Predominantly clicks without explaining; customer would not learn from observing.
Adapts to Beginner & Advanced Users
Adjusts depth, terminology, and pace based on the customer's level when prompted by the evaluator.
▲ Excellent
Seamlessly shifts language and depth mid-session when evaluator changes the "customer profile"; uses analogies for beginners and technical detail for advanced users.
● Meets Expectations
Adjusts with prompting; noticeable but acceptable shift in approach.
▼ Needs Improvement
One-speed training regardless of customer level; jargon-heavy with beginners.
Handles Difficult / Unexpected Questions
Responds confidently and accurately to curveball or advanced follow-up questions from the evaluator.
▲ Excellent
Answers accurately or says "let me verify that and follow up" — never guesses or bluffs; uses the question as a teaching moment.
● Meets Expectations
Answers correctly on most questions; minor uncertainty on edge cases.
▼ Needs Improvement
Guesses incorrectly; becomes flustered or avoids the question.
Troubleshooting Under Pressure
Diagnoses and resolves a simulated issue (scan failure, print error, software bug) in front of the "customer" without losing composure.
▲ Excellent
Narrates troubleshooting logic; reassures the customer; reaches correct resolution or appropriate escalation path.
● Meets Expectations
Resolves the issue correctly with some hesitation; maintains composure.
▼ Needs Improvement
Becomes flustered, provides incorrect fix, or escalates prematurely without attempting resolution.
Professionalism & Confidence
Presents with composure, appropriate dental vocabulary, organized delivery, and a customer-first tone.
▲ Excellent
Projects confident authority without arrogance; organized structure; patient with questions; represents Shining 3D brand appropriately.
● Meets Expectations
Professional; minor filler words or pacing issues but overall positive impression.
▼ Needs Improvement
Disorganized, dismissive of questions, or lacks confidence to the point of undermining trust.

⚠️ Critical Failure Areas — Automatic Certification Hold

Any item checked below prevents certification regardless of overall score. Check all that were observed during the assessment.

✕
Provided incorrect clinical guidance to the "customer"
E.g., wrong indications, contraindications, or tissue management advice that could cause patient harm.
✕
Unable to explain workflow rationale for any covered section
Could only perform steps; could not explain the WHY when directly asked.
✕
Repeated navigation errors across multiple sections
Three or more instances of being unable to locate core software functions without assistance.
✕
Unable to answer basic customer questions in any section
Guessed incorrectly or bluffed on fundamental product questions.
✕
Provided incorrect or unsafe troubleshooting recommendation
Advice that could damage hardware, corrupt data, or delay patient care.
✕
Unsafe device handling — scanner or printer
Dropped device, recommended incorrect disinfectants, skipped resin PPE, or handled components unsafely.
✕
Complete inability to recover from an unexpected question or error
Shut down, became defensive, or misled the customer rather than acknowledging the gap professionally.
